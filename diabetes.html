Understanding Types and Managing Diabetes

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition that occurs when the body either cannot produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it produces. Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood sugar (glucose), and imbalances can lead to elevated blood sugar levels.

Common Symptoms:

  1. Increased Thirst and Urination: Elevated blood sugar levels can lead to increased thirst and frequent urination.
  2. Fatigue: Insufficient glucose uptake by cells can result in fatigue.
  3. Unexplained Weight Loss: Despite increased hunger, weight loss may occur in individuals with diabetes.
  4. Complications: Untreated diabetes can lead to serious complications, including heart disease, stroke, kidney damage, and other vascular issues.
  5. Blurred Vision: High blood sugar levels can affect the eyes, causing blurred vision.

Understanding the type of diabetes, adopting a healthy lifestyle, and closely managing the condition is essential for individuals with diabetes to lead fulfilling, healthy lives. Regular consultations with healthcare professionals provide personalized guidance and support.
