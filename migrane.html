Migraine

What it is and what the symptoms are

What is a Migraine?

A migraine is not just a severe headache; it's a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, pulsating headaches that typically affect one side of the head. These throbbing pains can last for hours to days, significantly impacting a person's daily life.

What are the Symptoms of Migraines?

  • Throbbing Headache: The hallmark of Migraines is an intense, pulsating headache that can be debilitating.
  • Sensitivity to Light and Sound: Those suffering from migraine often become hypersensitive to light and sound, seeking solace in dark, quiet rooms during an attack.
  • Visual Disturbances: Migraines can cause visual disturbances, such as seeing shimmering lights or zigzag lines.
  • Fatigue and Irritability: Migraines can leave individuals feeling exhausted and irritable, impacting their overall well-being.
  • Nausea and Vomiting: Many individuals with migraines experience nausea and, in some cases, vomiting during an episode.
  • Aura: Some migraines come with auras - perceptual disturbances like flashes of light, blind spots, or tingling sensations in the limbs. However, not everyone experiences auras.

If you suspect you're dealing with migraines, consult our healthcare professionals who can provide personalized guidance and treatment options.
