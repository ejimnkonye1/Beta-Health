Understanding Genital Herpes: Navigating the Facts

What is Genital Herpes?

Genital herpes is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV). It primarily affects the genital and anal areas, leading to painful sores or blisters. Herpes is a lifelong infection with periodic outbreaks.

Key Facts about Genital Herpes:

  1. Two Types of HSV: HSV-1 and HSV-2 are the two main types of herpes simplex virus. While HSV-1 traditionally causes oral herpes (cold sores), it can also cause genital herpes through oral-genital contact.
  2. Symptoms: Symptoms may include painful sores, itching, and flu-like symptoms during the initial outbreak. Some people may have recurrent outbreaks, while others remain asymptomatic carriers.
  3. Transmission: Genital herpes spreads through intimate contact, including vaginal, anal, or oral sex. The virus can be transmitted even when sores are not present.

If you suspect you have genital herpes or have concerns about your sexual health, seek advice from a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and appropriate management.
